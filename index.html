About
I am currently a PhD Student at the Computer Science department of University of Liverpool. My research focus is on improving methods for structured prediction, transfer learning and multi-task learning in natural language. I'm apart of the Data Mining and Machine Learning (DMML) Research Group and the Natural Language Processing Group. My current focus right now is on latent coding in neural language models to improve scalability while maintaining performance.
Background
I studied Electronic Engineering at the National University of Ireland, Maynooth (NUIM) in Co.Kildare from Ireland, where I am originally from. Motivated by my undergraduate final year project, I began a MSc in Computer Science (Major in Data Analytics) at the National University of Ireland, Galway (NUIG). I then worked as a Research Assistant in the Unit for Natural Language Processing (UNLP) at the Insight Centre for Data Analytics. Soon after, I began my PhD on 1st March 2018.
